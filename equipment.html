Cameras:

Let us start with the basics of what you will need. Your phone or iPad can easily record a film, but if you want something better, try a Mirrorless Camera or a DSLR camcorder. The Panasonic G85/G80 is a good camera to start with. If you want to make documentaries, a camcorder will be your friend.

Lenses:

An affordable 50mm lens will do just the trick for starting out.

Sound Equipment:

Using sound equipment is especially important in film. Using the microphone built into the camera will not provide you with the best quality sound. Lavalier or Lav mics clip onto your clothing and would be a cheap option to use for starting off.

Tripods:

If you want your shots static with no panning to keep it simple, then a basic tripod will be good for you. If you want some motion, purchasing sliders or jibs (for vertical tracking shots) would be the best option.

Lights and Reflectors:

Once you get into the swing of things, having some light reflectors and lights such as GVM's would help you to control your light and add to the scene. You can also buy light gels to give some variety of color to your shots

Editing:

Finally, you will need a computer with editing software on it. The prime option is Adobe Premiere Pro. If you are tryiing to keep it cheap, using Final Cut Pro is also an option.
